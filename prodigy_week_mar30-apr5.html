  • URL clicks held flat at 10 despite reach dropping 46%. The concrete-dollar tweets ("$231 in 3 days", "$337 in 8 days") are converting at a much higher click-through rate than the abstract pitches that dominated prior weeks.

Areas for Improvement

  • Total impressions down 46% (1,884 → 1,020) and tweet volume down 37%. Three straight weeks of declining reach (3,263 → 1,884 → 1,020). Even with better per-tweet ER, the absolute volume of eyeballs is dropping fast and won't fix itself without a posting cadence change.
  • No tweet broke 150 impressions. Last week had a 277-impression top tweet; two weeks ago had multiple in the 200-400 range. The ceiling is collapsing, not just the average.
  • Zero bookmarks for the second week in a row. Two weeks ago there were 5. Nothing this week was educational or list-style enough for people to save — even the "where does the yield come from" thread (which should have been a saver) earned zero.
  • Zero new follows from any tweet this week. Across the entire prodigyfi history, follows only come from event invites, mention/reply hooks, and a viral provocative tweet (@lynk0x reply, 6,614 imp). None of this week's content matched those formats.
  • Heavy repetition of the "structured yield vs trading" framing — 5 of 12 tweets use the same "traders try to be right / yield gets paid" angle. Audience saturation is likely; per-tweet impressions are dropping as the framing repeats.

Recommendations for Next Week

  • Stop the reach decline: get back to 18-20 tweets/week. The cadence drop from 24 → 19 → 12 is the most likely cause of the impressions cliff. Algorithm rewards consistency — daily presence matters more than perfect tweets.
  • Lean into the dollar-outcome formula. "$231 in 3 days" was the best tweet of the week. Run a recurring "vault receipts" series — specific deposit amount, specific APY, specific dollar outcome, with a screenshot. Make it visual every time.
  • Break the "structured yield vs traders" loop. 5 tweets in one week with the same framing is too repetitive. Cut to 1-2/week max. Replace the rest with: vault performance screenshots, user testimonials, market data callouts, partner shoutouts.
  • Bring back saveable content. Bookmarks were 5 two weeks ago and 0 the last two weeks. People save lists, comparisons, how-tos, and explainers. Try one "5 things to know about structured yield" or "Prodigy vault types explained" post.
  • Add a follow-driving format weekly. The biggest follow-earning tweets historically were event invites (@ThetanutsFi mixer), provocative replies, and helpful comparisons. Aim for one "social hook" tweet per week alongside the product content.
