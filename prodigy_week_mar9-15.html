What Went Well

  • Engagement rate jumped to 9.9% (up from 6.6% last week) — people who see the tweets are interacting more
  • Likes nearly doubled week-over-week (66 vs 47), and reposts jumped from 17 to 24 — content is resonating better
  • The "defi myth" thread (918 impressions) was the top performer — educational/myth-busting content gets reach. Newsjacking the macro chaos ("US stocks dumping hard, oil exploding") also worked well at 230 impressions
  • Concrete examples with numbers land — "the leverage trap" post (219 imp, 6.85% ER) and "hot take: trying to time the market" (111 imp, 6.31% ER) both performed well with strong opinionated hooks
  • Volume was up (16 tweets vs 13 last week) without diluting overall quality

Areas for Improvement

  • Avg impressions dropped from 202 to 173 despite more total impressions — higher volume means some tweets are pulling the average down. Quality > quantity
  • URL clicks stayed flat at 7 (actually down from 8) — content is engaging but not driving traffic to the product. CTAs are getting lost or feel too tacked-on
  • Zero bookmarks this week (vs 1 last week) — bookmarks signal high-value "save for later" content. Educational posts this week were good but perhaps too bite-sized to bookmark
  • Thread tweets dropped off hard — the "defi myth" thread opener got 918 impressions but tweet 2 got 96 and tweet 3 got 74. That's a ~90% dropoff
  • Reply tweets and generic promos underperform — the @base reply (53 imp) and the 0DTE vault tweet (77 imp) are low-reach filler

Recommendations for Next Week

  • Fix the CTA problem. Engagement is strong but clicks are flat. Embed the link naturally into the narrative rather than appending it at the end. The "stablecoins costing you money" post was the only one with a URL click — it wove the CTA into the story.
  • Lean into formats that work. Opinionated hot takes, concrete examples with numbers ("deposit $10k, borrow $40k"), and newsjacking macro events all outperformed generic product tweets.
  • Rethink threads. 90% dropoff from tweet 1 to 2 means the audience isn't clicking through. Consider putting the key insight in a single tweet with an image, or make tweet 1 more of a cliffhanger.
  • Trim low-value tweets. Reply farming (@base) and generic vault promos drag the average down. Posting 12-13 higher-quality tweets may outperform 16 mixed ones.
