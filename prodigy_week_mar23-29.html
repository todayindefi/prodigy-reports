What Went Well

  • URL clicks hit a 3-week high (8 clicks). Up from 6 last week and 7 two weeks ago. The CTA strategy is slowly improving — the “0DTEs available here” CTA alone drove 3 clicks, showing that product-specific CTAs outperform generic ones.
  • The meme/humor format remains the top performer. “Crypto traders: analyzing charts for 8 hours” (206 imp, 8 likes, 9.71% ER) was the #1 tweet again. Relatable humor with an embedded product mention consistently outperforms straight product pitches.
  • “Hot take” hooks are generating consistent reach. Both hot-take tweets (204 imp, 129 imp) landed in the top 3. The educational angle — explaining funding rates, showing how fear creates opportunity — resonates better than “earn X% APY” product posts.
  • The “two ways to approach yield” tweet (76 imp) had a 21% ER — the highest ER of any content tweet this week. Short, clear comparisons (short-term vs longer-tenor) with an image attached drove 5 likes and 3 reposts from a small audience.

Areas for Improvement

  • No breakout tweet this week. The ceiling was 206 impressions vs last week’s 1,233. Two weeks ago peaked at 1,008. The account hasn’t produced a 500+ impression tweet this week. The educational explainer format (“where does yield come from”, “defi myth: you need leverage”) that drives breakouts was absent.
  • Zero new followers for a second consecutive week. 19 tweets, 1,537 impressions, and not a single follow. The content is engaging the existing audience but failing to convert new eyeballs. The account grew by 0 followers across 43 tweets in 2 weeks.
  • Zero bookmarks. Down from 5 last week. Bookmarks signal “save-worthy” content (guides, explainers, threads). This week was heavy on short takes and light on deep educational content that people reference later.
  • Avg impressions continue a 3-week decline: 204 → 126 → 81. Reach per tweet has dropped 60% in two weeks. This suggests either algorithm headwinds, posting time issues, or content fatigue from the repetitive “earn yield” angle.
  • Parent content tweets still aren’t driving clicks themselves. Only 1 URL click came from a parent tweet (out of ~1,300 parent impressions). The CTA-reply strategy is doing the click-driving work, but 0.08% CTR on parent tweets means the link isn’t compelling enough in the main post.
