What Went Well

  • Opinion & meme tweets dominated. The "leverage is expensive insurance" take hit 18.18% engagement rate and the Dr. ProdigyFi meme hit 15.2% — both far above the week's 7.64% average. Short, personality-driven content is the top-performing format.
  • Referral program thread carried reach. The head tweet alone pulled 780 impressions — nearly half the week's total. Product news still gets distributed when it's genuinely new.
  • Best avg impressions per tweet in 2 weeks. At 150 avg imp/tweet, up from 93 the prior week. Content quality is translating to better per-post reach.
  • URL clicks trending up. 0.30% CTR vs 0.06% two weeks ago. Still low in absolute terms but directionally right.

What Could Be Improved

  • Community reply engagement dropped to zero. Feb 10-16 had 99 replies, Feb 17-23 had 73 — this week had none. Those reply-heavy weeks had the highest avg impressions on original tweets (280 and 172). Reply engagement likely boosts algo visibility and gets the handle in front of new audiences.
  • Thread CTA tweets are dead weight. Both "try it here" tweets got 35 and 52 impressions, 0 likes combined. Bare link CTAs get buried by the algorithm. Embed links in substantive tweets or give the CTA tweet real copy.
  • Only 1 new follower all week. Engagement is coming from existing audience. No discovery-oriented content (quote-tweeting bigger accounts, trending topic takes, collabs) to attract new followers.
  • Nearly zero bookmarks. Content is consumable but not save-worthy. Educational explainers, data visuals, and comparison threads tend to get bookmarked and have longer shelf life.

Recommendation

Content quality is at its best — opinion and meme formats are clearly winning. The gap is community engagement. The highest-performing week (Feb 10-16: 280 avg imp/tweet) paired 17 original tweets with ~100 replies to other accounts. Bringing back even 30-50 targeted replies/week alongside the current content quality should push reach back toward those levels. Additionally, kill bare-link CTA tweets and replace with substantive copy that embeds the link.
