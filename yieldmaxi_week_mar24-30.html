  • Engagement rate recovered to 9.6% from the declining trend (15.8% → 9.2% → 8.0%). The mid-tier tweets (125-199 imp) had strong ER (3.5%-11.2%), showing the smaller tweets are pulling their weight.

Areas for Improvement

  • Total likes dropped 16% (37 vs 44) despite much higher reach. The like-to-impression ratio fell — especially on the two big tweets (8 likes on 2,102 imp = 0.4%, 9 likes on 1,864 imp = 0.5%). High reach tweets are getting seen but not loved. The content may be informative but not emotionally resonant enough to earn likes.
  • Only 6 tweets posted vs 8-10 in prior weeks. While per-tweet performance improved, total volume is down 25%. There are missing days (no tweets on Mar 28-29) which means lost opportunities for compounding feed presence.
  • The Mar 30 tweet is underperforming early (23 imp). The funding rates tweet is getting minimal traction — no image, no tag, no external reference. Compared to the two top tweets that both had hooks (price data, fear narrative), this one opens with a generic market observation.
  • Bookmarks declined from 7 last week to 4. Despite having higher-quality content, fewer people are saving posts. The curation/resource posts (Resolv list) generated saves, but the product-focused posts generated zero.
  • Zero new follows from the two biggest tweets. 2,102 + 1,864 = nearly 4,000 impressions with 0 follows between them. The Resolv tweet earned 1 follow, but the ETH comparison tweet — despite 10 URL clicks — converted nobody. The profile/bio may not be compelling enough for cold visitors.
