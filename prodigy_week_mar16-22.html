Areas for Improvement

  • Content tweet avg impressions still dropped. Even excluding reply CTAs, the 17 content tweets averaged ~159 impressions vs 201 last week. The top tweet (1,218) carried the total — median content tweet performance was closer to 89 impressions.
  • Reply CTA reach is inconsistent. Some reply CTAs get 45-49 impressions (decent), others get 2-16 (invisible). The "deposit here:" reply on the sell-high explainer got 2 impressions — essentially no one saw it. The ones that worked better ("earn ~200% here:", "up to 200% APY here:") included a specific yield number in the text.
  • Reply CTAs are the primary click driver (3 of 6 clicks) but still low volume. The parent content tweets themselves are driving almost no clicks (3 clicks from ~2,600 combined impressions). The CTA strategy is working in concept but the conversion funnel is thin.
  • Outbound reply tweets flopped. The @tradertheory reply (35 imp), @fintechfrank reply (23 imp), and @iamdisplacement reply (54 imp, 0 likes) got minimal traction. Long-form product pitches in other people's replies aren't reaching beyond the original poster's audience.
  • The "when the market moves like this" tweet (10 imp) was a standalone with a link, not a reply CTA. It read like a CTA without any setup — a parent tweet that had no parent content. It performed the worst of all non-reply tweets.

Recommendations for Next Week

  • Improve reply CTA copy. The reply CTAs that include a specific number ("earn ~200%", "up to 200% APY") get 45-49 impressions and drive clicks. The generic ones ("deposit here:", "try yours here:") get 2-16 impressions and zero clicks. Always include a specific yield figure or dollar amount in the reply CTA.
  • Add the link to the parent tweet too. The #1 tweet (1,218 imp) had no link at all — it relied entirely on a reply CTA that far fewer people saw. Embedding a link in the parent tweet itself (even mid-text) would capture the audience at peak attention rather than hoping they scroll to the reply.
  • Make the explainer format a weekly staple. The #1 tweet (1,218 imp) and #2 tweet (196 imp, 13.78% ER) were both "here's how X works" posts. Do 2-3 of these per week — "how sell-high works", "what happens at expiry", "where does premium come from", etc. These earn bookmarks and reach.
  • Cut the outbound reply pitches. The @tradertheory (35 imp), @fintechfrank (23 imp), and @iamdisplacement (54 imp, 0 likes) replies aren't converting. These long-form product pitches in other people's threads aren't reaching their audience. Try shorter, more natural engagement instead of selling in replies.
  • Pair scenario tweets with real vault data. The "pov: holding 10 ETH" format works (121 imp, 3 reposts). Make it even more compelling by adding actual current vault APRs and real dollar amounts. "This vault returned $X this week" is more clickable than "earn up to 200% APR."
